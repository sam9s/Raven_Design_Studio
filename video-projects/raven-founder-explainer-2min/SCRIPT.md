# SCRIPT — Raven Solutions Founder Explainer (2 min)

**Status:** Draft v1 — for Sammy to rewrite in his own voice before recording.

**Target length:** 1:55–2:10 (approximately 280–320 words at conversational pace ~160–170 wpm).

**Tone:** Calm, premium, founder-led, Indian-SME friendly. Speak as if to one founder across the table, not a room. Short sentences. Breathing room. No hype vocabulary ("revolutionary," "unleash," "10x"). Read it slowly — the motion graphics will fill any pauses.

**Delivery notes:**

- Pause briefly after every heading marker in square brackets — those are your beat changes. Half a second of breath is gold.

## The script

**[HOOK — 0:00–0:12]**

> "If you run a growing business in India, you know the feeling.
> Customer follow-ups happen in spreadsheets. Orders move between tools that don't talk to each other.
> Manual work quietly piles up. And so do the workarounds."

**[WHO I AM — 0:12–0:30]**

> "I'm Samret Singh. I run Raven Solutions.
> Eighteen years ago I started building enterprise systems, and I've spent that time on what I call the unglamorous half of technology — automation, workflows, integrations.
> The things that actually execute real work inside a business."

**[WHY RAVEN EXISTS — 0:30–0:58]**

> "Here's what I believe: automation should do the work. Not visualize it in a dashboard. Not answer questions about it in a chat. Actually execute it.
> Most businesses I meet are stuck with tools that stop one step short of that.
> So we started Raven Solutions in August twenty twenty-five with a simple idea —
> build automation that executes real business work, across sales, support, and operations. Not another dashboard."

**[GREST — 1:00–1:45]**

> "Our flagship engagement is GREST — India's most trusted premium refurbished device brand.
> We're not building one automation for them. We're building their entire operations backbone — the GREST ERP.
> Phase one is the Inventory Management System. Ninety-five percent complete, currently in UAT, going live in days.
> Then D2C operations. Then B2C retail. Then B2B. Every channel, one module at a time, running on one system.
> We're not rebuilding on day one. We're building alongside the business."

**[WAYVEDA + NATURE MANIA — 1:45–2:00]**

> "And when a business needs a fast, focused win, we deliver those too.
> Wayveda needed Shopify and Shiprocket reconciled into one operational view. Closed in under forty-eight hours.
> Nature Mania needed multi-courier visibility across two hundred and fifty orders a day. Same window."

**[CLOSE — 2:00–2:12]**

> "Founder-led. Direct communication. No account management layers.
> If your business has a workflow problem, let's talk."

## What to do with this

1. Read it out loud, twice. Mark anything that feels stiff.
2. Rewrite those lines. Your phrasing wins.
3. When the script reads smooth end-to-end, record it. Don't chase perfection per take — two or three clean passes of the whole script is fine; I'll pick the best sections per beat.
4. Save as `assets/voiceover.mp3` (or `.wav`) and tell me it's ready.

## Recording tips (brief — you've done YouTube VOs before)

- Quiet room. No fan or AC roar.
- Mic ~15 cm from your mouth, slightly off-axis.
- 44.1 kHz, 192 kbps MP3 or 16-bit WAV is plenty.
- Smile while you speak — it warms the voice even without camera.
- Pause generously between beats. Silence edits out cleanly; rushing doesn't.
